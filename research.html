Research Focus

Time-Resolved Structural Biology

Our primary research focuses on understanding how biological macromolecules function by capturing their movements in real-time. Traditional structural biology often provides static snapshots. Using Time-Resolved Crystallography and advanced spectroscopic methods, we can visualize enzymes in action at atomic resolution.


Key Areas of Investigation:

  • DNA Repair Mechanisms: Investigating the continuous dynamic mapping of DNA repair processes by photoenzymes, such as photolyases and bifunctional cryptochromes.
  • Photoreceptor Activation: Uncovering new activation modes and structural dynamics in light-sensitive proteins.
  • Methodology Development: Advancing techniques like Serial Femtosecond Crystallography (SFX) and developing new matrices (e.g., Guanosine hydrogels) for efficient protein sample delivery.
